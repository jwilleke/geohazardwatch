## Tsunamis

A tsunami is a series of ocean waves generated by the sudden displacement of a large
volume of water — most often by a submarine earthquake, but also by landslides,
volcanic eruptions, or coastal collapse. In the open ocean the waves are barely
noticeable; as they reach shallow water near shore they slow and pile up, and can
arrive as a fast-rising surge many meters high.

## Alert Levels

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Warning | Dangerous coastal flooding and powerful currents likely or underway. Move to high ground. |
| Advisory | Strong currents or waves dangerous to those in or near the water. Stay out of the water. |
| Watch | A distant earthquake may later cause a tsunami; stay alert for updates. |
| Information | A minor or distant event; no action needed for most coasts. |
